Who we are

About RoomReset

A home decor and organization guide built for people who want their space to feel calm, beautiful, and completely their own, without needing a large budget or a designer to get there.

RoomReset was created out of a simple frustration: most home decor content online is either aimed at people with unlimited budgets, or it is so generic that it could apply to any room in any country and ends up being useful to nobody in particular. We wanted something more honest than that. A guide that starts from the reality most people are actually living in, a modest amount of money, a small or imperfect space, and a genuine desire to make it feel better without turning it into a renovation project.

Every article on this site is written with one question in mind: what is the single most effective thing someone can do to improve this room today, with what they can reasonably afford? That question shapes everything from the products we recommend to the way we explain each idea. We never recommend something because it looks good in a photoshoot. We recommend it because it genuinely earns its place in a real room.

"A beautiful home is not about how much you spend. It is about how intentionally you choose."

What We Cover

RoomReset covers the full range of home improvement that does not require a contractor or a credit card limit you would rather not think about. Our content falls into six main areas, each chosen because it represents a part of the home where a small investment of time and money tends to produce a disproportionately large improvement in how the space looks and feels.

Room Ideas

Full bedroom, living room, and kitchen guides that walk through the most impactful changes you can make to each space, from lighting to textiles to furniture arrangement.

Organization

Practical systems for fridges, closets, laundry rooms, and desks that look good and stay that way, because an organized space is only useful if you can actually maintain it.

Budget Decor

Curated product roundups sorted by price point so you always know exactly what fits your budget before you start reading, with honest notes on what is worth it and what is not.

By Style

Aesthetic specific guides for cottagecore, moody, minimalist, Art Deco, and more, so you can build toward a look that feels consistent rather than randomly assembled.

Small Spaces

Ideas specifically designed for apartments, shared spaces, and rooms where every square foot counts, including reading nooks, balconies, and single room studio setups.

Shop Picks

Handpicked product lists from Amazon India organized by room and style, each chosen because it delivers good quality at a price that makes sense for the average home budget.

How We Choose What We Recommend

Every product recommendation on RoomReset goes through the same basic test before it makes it onto the site. Does it do what it claims to do, does it look good in a real home setting rather than just a staged photo, is the price genuinely reasonable for what you are getting, and does it have enough reviews from real buyers to give us confidence that it holds up over time. Products that pass all four questions make the cut. Products that fail any one of them do not appear here, regardless of commission rates or brand partnerships.

We also update our recommendations regularly. Home products change, prices shift, and things go in and out of stock. An out of date recommendation that leads you to a discontinued product or a price that no longer reflects reality is not useful to anyone, so we treat our articles as living documents rather than one and done posts.
